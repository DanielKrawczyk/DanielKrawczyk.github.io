About me:

 My name is Daniel Krawczyk. I am a Junior Front-End Developer and I started my adventure with programming in 2020. I am not short of ambition, but full of willing to continue learning and develop myself in this direction.
 Currently I am acquiring knowledge about noSQL databases and developing my own project on GitHub, which at the moment offers a playable demo. A second version of this project is also being developed, which will use the Angular framework instead of the React library. In addition, as a hobby, I help with the work on the modification called Golden Gate to Gothic II (as a level-designer) and learn how to use the Unreal Engine.
 My interests in IT and computers started from an early age. In the moments of play of cult titles such as Gothic, Morrowind, Heroes III, Stronghold, Anno, Baldur's Gate or Diablo II, I slowly aroused my curiosity and fascination with computers and IT. I used to ask myself "How that games are made?". Years passed, technology was moving forward, and I saw more and more beautiful and advanced wonders. I started to get into computer science harder and harder, until finally curiosity won over reason and after a few months I took my first laptop apart and put in back together. And surprisingly it continued to work!
 It did not end with disassembling laptops. I rummaged around the system wherever I could. I looked in awe at every corner where the average user would not be too eager to look. At one point, I also became interested in computer viruses. However, I limited this to YouTube videos. Until I got my hands on more powerful hardware on which I could install VirtualBox and test various threats in a completely safe and isolated manner. This futher expanded my learning opportunities and I didn't have to worry about breaking something by accident!
 I went abroad to work for a vacation in high school. With the money I earned, I bought a laptop, which made me even more attached to computer science. I learned some minor repairs and maintenance, tried my hand at the level-design on the ZenGin Engine, where I am currently helping with the work on the Gothic II add-on called Golden Gate and took my part in the trial cybersecurity course.
 Eventually, I also got interested in programming. This is how I started a development course that offered CodeHub. My enormous ambitions drew me into creating websites and after several months of intensive study, I was able to complete the course and gain the knowledge needed to start a new adventure in my life. I am going to develop and broaden my skills to become a Full Stack Developer in the future!
